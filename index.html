Cake · Flagship

Neem Cake & Powder

Garden & GreenhouseField & Plantation

The dense, plant-derived manure left when neem seeds are pressed for oil. Worked into pots, beds or open fields, it releases nitrogen and organic matter slowly — for steadier feeding, richer soil life and stronger growth season after season, while helping every other input work harder. Also milled into a fine, versatile powder for potting mixes, composts and custom blends.

Karanja (Honge) Cake

Garden & GreenhouseField & Plantation

Pressed from the seeds of the Pongamia tree — one of India’s most trusted natural soil manures. Dense with organic matter and slow-release nutrients, it feeds the soil steadily, enriches its structure and supports strong root growth, holding nutrients and moisture so plants are fed evenly over weeks.

Forms powder · granule   Packing 25 / 50 kg or repackable   Specs on request

The Soil

Some things don’t need reinventing.

Long before fertiliser came in bags, Indian growers fed their fields with what the land gave back — the pressed cakes of neem, karanja and castor, returned to the soil after their oils were drawn.

From a nursery’s potting bench to a plantation’s open rows, what we ship does one quiet, dependable thing — it feeds the soil, not just the season.

We are not the oldest name in this trade — and we don’t pretend to be. What we offer is care: in what we source, how we grade it, and how it reaches you.
